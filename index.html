01. My Story

10 Schools. 10 Cities. One Thread.

I grew up moving — 10 schools across India, from metropolitan cities to small towns. Each move meant new classmates, new cultures, new languages, new ways of seeing the world. What could have been disorienting became my foundation: learning to connect with anyone, anywhere.

India's diversity taught me that understanding people isn't about finding similarities — it's about being genuinely curious about differences. That skill has shaped everything I do: how I build products, how I teach, how I lead.

From Learning to Giving Back

When I cleared JEE Advanced and joined IIT Gandhinagar, I knew it was time to give back. I became a Student Guide, helping freshers navigate the overwhelming transition to college life. Later, as a Peer-Assisted Learning Mentor, I worked with differently-abled students — learning that teaching is really about meeting people where they are.

As a Student Representative for Career Development Services, I led placement cycles for my batch — understanding that connecting talent with opportunity is its own form of impact.

The Discipline of Sport

I've held a racquet since I was 3. Learned to swim at 2. Sports isn't a hobby for me — it's a philosophy. The discipline, the pressure, the need to stay calm when the set is on the line — these aren't just athletic skills. They're life skills.

At IIT Gandhinagar, I didn't just play — I founded the Lawn Tennis community. Trained students, especially women, to compete. Organized intra-college events to build engagement. Won medals for the institute. And was awarded the Institute Gold Medal for Outstanding Performance in Outdoor Sports.

"Preparation beats talent. The best players stay calm when the match is on the line. I bring that mindset to everything."
